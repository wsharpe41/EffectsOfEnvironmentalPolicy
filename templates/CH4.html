Kyoto Protocol Background

The Kyoto Protocol is an international agreement which became effective in 2005 and was meant to extend the UN Framework Convention on Climate Change (also discussed). The goal of this agreement is to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and thus reduce the impact of global warming. The Kyoto Protocol specifically focused on seven GHGs: CO2, N2O, CH4, PFCs, HFCs, NF3, and SF6. This agreement is unique in that it specifically placed stricter guidelines on more developed countries. There were two “commitment periods” for this agreement, the first was from 2008 to 2012 and the second from 2012 to 2020.
