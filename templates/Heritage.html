World Heritage Convention Background

The World Heritage Convention (1972) is a treaty which established UNESCO world heritage sites with the goal of natural and cultural preservation. It has been ratified by almost all countries globally (194) and also established the World Heritage Fund which is meant to be used to protect heritage sites.
